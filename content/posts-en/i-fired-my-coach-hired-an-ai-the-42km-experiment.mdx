A few months ago I was in "amateur runner hell." I had a plan stuck to the fridge that said: *"Tuesday: 12km with Fartlek."*

But my real Tuesday was:
1. Max sick → took him to the doctor.

That's when I said: **"Enough."**

I decided to hack the system.

### Adaptive Training Protocol with AI

Here's how I built my own system. It's not magic. It's data engineering applied to the body. I used **Gemini** (you can use Claude or ChatGPT) and created a specific "Gem" with these 3 steps:

**1. Context Ingestion (The Master Prompt):** Don't ask "give me a marathon plan." That's amateur. You need to give it your hard constraints. My initial prompt included:

> *"I'm X years old. I weigh X kg. My VO2 Max is X. I can train 4 days, not 6. My heavy work days are Thursdays. My best recent half marathon time was X. Goal: Buenos Aires Marathon in 16 weeks."*

That's the simple version — the more detailed you make it, the better. Take that output to an Excel or Notion. That's your map, but the map isn't the territory.

**2. The Daily Feedback Loop:** Every morning, I feed the AI my recovery metrics (HRV and Sleep from Garmin) and my schedule.

> *Prompt: "I slept badly today (Score 40) and have 3 hours of back-to-back meetings. Adjust today's training so I don't burn out."*

**3. The Live Recalculation ("Recalculating..."):** If I couldn't train on Tuesday, the AI doesn't make me feel guilty. It recalculates the weekly volume and redistributes it.

The secret: I manage 3 horizons (Optimistic, Realistic, Pessimistic). If the week gets complicated, the AI switches to the "Pessimistic" plan to save minimum volume without breaking me. The important thing is to arrive at the start line and complete the marathon in the worst case.
