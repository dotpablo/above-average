The problem isn't that you lack time. It's that your daily architecture is broken.

Last week we talked about **momentum**. About not putting zeros. About building mental calluses. About constructing that habit that changes your life.

But after that piece, several people asked me the same question: *"All well and good, Pablo. I've got momentum — but how do I keep it without ending up in the hospital?"*

The million-dollar question. The answer you want to hear: "You need perfect balance." The real answer: **Balance doesn't exist.**

72% of working parents report that their work constantly interferes with their family life. In practical terms, it looks like this:
- You arrive home physically, but your head is still mentally answering emails.
- Your kids tell you about their day and you nod on autopilot (you didn't hear a word).
- The gym waits for "when there's spare time" (spoiler: there's never spare time).
- You read the same page 4 times because your mental RAM is full.

I was part of that 72% for years. Today I'm not. And it's not because I achieved "balance." It's because I stopped using the scale.

---

### THE BALANCE TRAP

Most working parents believe the solution is the famous "work-life balance" (sounds nice). They think if they divide the day into equal portions, they can give 100% in each area.

This creates a brutal cycle: You try to give 100% at the office (8-9 hours) → You arrive home wrecked → You try to force "quality time" with guilt → You sleep badly to have "time for yourself" → You repeat the cycle but more tired.

**My breaking point:** When Max asked me: *"Dad, why are you always tired?"* it hit me like a train. I realized I wasn't building an *Above Average* life. I was trying to survive an *Average* life with a nicer schedule.

Balance assumes all domains need the same energy every day. That's a lie.

---

### THE SYSTEM: DOMAIN STACK

Forget dividing the day. The secret is **stacking** your 4 domains in an order that maximizes your energy, not your time. It's not *time management*, it's *energy management*.

Here's the exact system I use:

### STEP 1: IDENTIFY YOUR ANCHOR

Choose one single domain that, if you execute it well, makes everything else easier or irrelevant. For me it's **Health**.

If I train before the sun rises:
1. My energy for the day is already guaranteed.
2. I've already won before the chaos begins.
3. Everything else has a clear mind behind it.

If I win the morning with my anchor, I win the day. The rest is downhill.

### STEP 2: STACK, DON'T SEPARATE

The mistake is seeing your life as separate compartments. "Now I'm an employee," "Now I'm a dad." You have to stack the domains so one feeds the next. Sometimes even in parallel if you work remotely.

**My real stack:**
1. Health (5-6:30am) — charges everything else.
2. Family (7-8am breakfast, 6-9pm evenings) — non-negotiable.
3. Work (8am-5pm) — with full energy after the anchor.
4. Personal projects (5-6pm) — what I build for the future.

See the difference? I don't fight for time. I flow with energy. If I tried to train at 7pm, after working, I'd arrive home dead, train badly, and have no patience for my kids. The order of factors **does** alter the product.

### STEP 3: DISCRIMINATE WITHOUT GUILT (THE HARD PART)

This is where most people fail. To be *Above Average* at what matters, you have to be "bad" at what's irrelevant. You have to choose what you're going to sacrifice.

Average wants "options and freedom" — you have to become a defender of your time or this fails.

*Average* sacrifices things unwillingly (sleep, health, presence) and feels guilty. *Above Average* sacrifices consciously and sleeps peacefully.

**My non-negotiable "NOs":**
- **Endless scrolling:** If I'm having lunch I use that time to read or advance on personal projects.
- **I don't watch TV:** I cancelled cable. I was a series binge-watcher — now I watch a movie monthly with my wife in fragments. Zero news, zero channel surfing.
- **I don't go to generic "networking" events:** If there's no clear ROI, I don't go.
- **I don't stay up late:** 6 hours of sleep are sacred. Without sleep there's no anchor.
- **People who don't add value:** Moving away from people who aren't on the same wavelength is the best thing you can do for yourself.

Am I antisocial? No. I'm focused. I know what I'm missing. But I'm crystal clear on what I'm gaining. You have to learn the art of discrimination.

### THE RESULTS (REAL DATA, NOT HYPE)

Since I killed "balance" and started using the *Stack*:
- **Career:** I work 8 hours but perform like the 12-hour days of before. Productivity +40%.
- **Family:** I have breakfast and dinner with Max and Mía almost every day. Real presence, no phone at the table.
- **Health:** 5-6 weekly training sessions consistently. Energy 8.5/10 stable all day.
- **Mind:** ~15 books per year (before I was lucky to read 3).

I didn't sacrifice any important domain. I just eliminated the fat.

### YOUR WEEKLY ACTION PLAN

Don't copy my routine. Copy the logic.

**MONDAY-TUESDAY: THE ANCHOR** Ask yourself: *What one thing, if I do it well, improves everything else?* Usually it's Health or Mind. Identify it. That's your priority #1. If that doesn't happen, the day doesn't start.

**WEDNESDAY-THURSDAY: THE DESIGN** Write your ideal order. Not by clock, but by energy flow.
- What charges you? (Goes first)
- What drains you? (Goes when you have a full tank)
- What recovers you? (Goes at the end)

**FRIDAY-SUNDAY: THE "NOT TO DO" LIST** Write 3 things you're going to stop doing this week.
- Infinite IG scroll?
- That Tuesday meeting that could be an email?
- Automatic Netflix after dinner? Eliminate them without guilt. You're buying freedom.
