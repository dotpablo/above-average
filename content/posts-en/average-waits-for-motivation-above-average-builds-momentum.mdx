Motivation lasts 14 days. Momentum lasts a lifetime.

### The Confession Nobody Makes

I was an elite procrastinator.

Not the "leave everything for the last minute" type. The dangerous type. The type that *knows* they can do more, but chooses not to.

For years, my favorite trap was this: **"I have time. I'll get it done anyway."**

And it worked. I'd study the night before and pass. I'd deliver the project 5 minutes before the deadline.

That pattern made me seem *reliable*... but I was mediocre.

My brain was selling me a story: "You're efficient under pressure."

The reality: I was *Average*. Like everyone else.

But my favorite way to procrastinate wasn't Netflix. It was **mental masturbation.**

I'd spend 3 hours watching "The 7 Habits of Highly Productive People" videos and that type of guru content.

I'd read 47 motivational quotes on Instagram and feel powerful for 5 seconds.

I'd listen to *biohacking* podcasts while eating french fries at McDonald's.

At the end of the day: same place. Same couch. Same life. Waiting for the miracle that would magically change me.

The difference between "researching" and "executing" is the same as between reading a gym manual and lifting the bar.

One makes you *feel* productive. The other makes you *be* productive.

Today, this trap is more lethal than ever.

Algorithms are designed to give you dopamine without effort. Every scroll, every 10-second video, trains your brain to prefer passive consumption (the excuse) over difficult action (execution).

Average consumes content about productivity.

> Above Average *executes* — and momentum does the rest.

### The Statistic That Kills Motivation

Strava (the athlete social network) did more than show your contacts' workouts — it also analyzed millions of training sessions and discovered something brutal:

**"Quitter's Day" exists.**

It's the day most people abandon their New Year's resolutions.

When is it? The second Friday of January.

> **Motivation lasts, on average, 14 days.**

Two weeks. 336 hours. After that, 80% of people return to exactly where they started.

Average builds their life on an emotion with an expiration date.

Like basing your economy on a bill that expires in 2 weeks.

I don't get up at 4:50am because I'm motivated from reading "The 5 AM Club."

90% of the time, I'm not. 90% of the time, my brain gives me 47 reasons to stay in bed.

> The difference is simple: **motivation is no longer part of my system.**

### The System: Momentum > Motivation

If motivation is the enemy, what's the system?

It's called **Momentum.**

And it's backed by physics, not cheap motivational quotes.

**Newton's First Law:**
- An object at rest tends to stay at rest.
- An object in motion tends to stay in motion.

Translation:
- Average on the couch → needs A LOT of energy to start (motivation).
- Above Average in motion → needs LITTLE energy to continue (discipline).

Average watches an inspiring video, trains for 2 days, burns out, and returns to rest.

> Above Average uses a small activation force to start, and then **uses momentum to never stop.**

### The Goggins Mindset

David Goggins is an extreme (but for me, exceptional). And whether you like him or not, his central concept is 100% Above Average:

**"Callousing the Mind"** is the concept Goggins explains in his book "Can't Hurt Me" and I find it exceptional for understanding the difference between mediocre and Above Average.

Goggins explains it this way:

> *"It takes twenty years to gain twenty years of experience, and the only way to move beyond your 40 percent is to callous your mind, day after day. Which means you'll have to chase pain like it's your damn job!"*

The 40% Rule is Goggins's belief that when your mind tells you you're "done," "exhausted," "broken," or "can't go on" — you're actually only using **40% of your true capacity.**

Goggins says our brains have a **"Governor"** (like a car engine's rev limiter). It's a self-preservation mechanism.

The Governor's only job is to keep you safe and comfortable.

It's the survival mechanism that evolved to avoid risk, pain, and death. Its job is to **keep you *Average***.
- That's why Average looks to "feel good" before doing something.
- While Above Average does things *precisely when* they don't want to.

Every time you get up when the alarm goes off → you build a callus.

Every time you train in the rain → you build a callus.

Every time you choose the book over Netflix → you build a callus.

> **Mental masturbation softens your mind and keeps you below your 40%.** **Execution calloused it.**

Goggins discovered (especially during Navy SEALs Hell Week) that the Governor keeping you mediocre is just a voice.

It's a programmed suggestion we have at the subconscious level — not an impossible brick wall.

The only way to "go beyond" is to reprogram that voice. And that only happens through sustained discomfort over time.

People ask me: "Pablo, how do you motivate yourself?"

I don't motivate myself. I silence the voice that tells me to stop. I execute the system. Yesterday's momentum does 80% of today's work.

### The Deadly Trap: "100% or 0%"

The biggest momentum killer is the "All or Nothing" mindset.

Real examples:
- "I feel bad today, I can't run my 10km, so I'll do nothing."
- "I ate a cookie, I ruined my diet, so I'll order a pizza."
- "I don't have 1 hour to work on my project, so Netflix it is."

They put a **ZERO.**

And ZERO is the silent killer of momentum.

0% forces you to need "motivation" to start again tomorrow.

It's like resetting your progress to level 1 every day.

### The Brutal Math of 1%

Above Average understands that **1% is infinitely better than 0%.**

The math of the compound effect doesn't lie:

**Scenario 1: You stay the same (1.00) for a year**
- 1^365 = 1
- Result: You're exactly the same person.

**Scenario 2: You improve 1% each day**
- 1.01^365 = 37.78
- Result: You're almost **38 times better** than when you started a year ago.

**Scenario 3: You do 1% less each day (put "zeros")**
- 0.99^365 = 0.03
- Result: **You go extinct.**

> A "zero day" isn't a zero. It's a 0.99. It's the road to extinction.

> **Your only job is to NOT PUT A ZERO.**

### The Action: "Don't Break the Chain 2.0"

This week, your goal isn't to be perfect. Your goal is **not to put a zero** in that thing you want to do.

### The system:
1. Take a calendar — physical or digital.
2. Every day you execute (even minimally), put an X.
3. Your only job: protect the streak.

The X doesn't mean "I completed a perfect task." The X means: **"I won. I didn't put a zero."**

### Real example: "I want to train"

**Monday (Good Day):**
- You run 10km or did 45' of exercise → **X**

**Tuesday (Chaotic Day):**
- You wake up late, kids sick, surprise meeting.
- You do 10 minutes of burpees in the living room → **X**

**Wednesday (Injured):**
- Your knee hurts.
- You do 15 minutes of mobility and stretching → **X**

Average would have put "0" on Tuesday and Wednesday. Would have killed the momentum. Would have needed "motivation" on Thursday to start again. The weekend comes and it's over — the infinite cycle of the mediocre who never continues anything.

Above Average put an X. Keeps moving. On Thursday, getting up is 10 times easier.

> **The streak is what matters.** Not perfection. Consistency.
