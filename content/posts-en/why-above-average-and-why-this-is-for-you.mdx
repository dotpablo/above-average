### THE STATISTIC: AVERAGE MEDIOCRITY

Average isn't "bad people."

It's the default route when you don't actively choose something else — when you operate on autopilot.

Look at these statistics:
- 70% are overweight.
- Only 0.5% have run a marathon.
- 43% don't read a single book per year.
- The average person watches 4+ hours of TV.
- Only 1% of marathoners run sub-3:30.

Me:
- 6 weekly training sessions.
- 19 marathons completed (last one: 3h14') — top 10% in my age group.
- 20+ books per year.
- No TV.

*(I'm not a genius. I have systems.)*

### THE ACTION (for today)

Choose ONE average statistic that bothers you.

It could be:
- "The average person doesn't read"
- "The average person is overweight"
- "The average person works without strategy"

Now ask yourself:

***"What decision can I eliminate so I'm not average in this?"***

Examples:

### You want to read more:
- *Book on the nightstand (not on the shelf)*
- *10 pages before touching your phone*

### You want to train:
- *Workout clothes next to the bed (ready the night before)*
- *Alarm far from reach*
- *Out the door without overthinking*

### You want to work better:
- 3 priorities at the start of the day (not inbox)
- Deep work before meetings
- Say NO to everything else

> Average improvises. Above Average has systems.
