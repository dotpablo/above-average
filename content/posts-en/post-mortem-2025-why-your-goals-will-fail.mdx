### The "Goals" Bug (Scott Adams)

In his book *How to Fail at Almost Everything and Still Win Big*, Scott Adams destroys the idea of goals with an engineer's logic:

> *"Losers have goals. Winners have systems."*

The structural problem with a goal (e.g., "Run a marathon") is that you live in a state of **constant failure** until the moment you achieve it. And if you get injured the day before, all the effort feels like a system crash. It's fragile.

A **System** (e.g., "Run 30 minutes every day at 6 AM") changes the reward. Every time you go out, the code ran well. You won. The system gives you daily dopamine. The marathon stops being the objective and becomes an inevitable side effect.

### Antifragility: Using the Chaos (Nassim Taleb)

This is where most professional parents fail. We look for "stability," but life with kids and corporate work is pure chaos.

Nassim Taleb explains in Antifragile that your 2025 review isn't to punish yourself. It's to extract data.

Every mistake of this year, every day you didn't follow through, every argument you lost — is **Data**. If you ignore that data, 2026 will have the same *bugs* as 2025.
